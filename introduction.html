Introduction to Burnout

Definition of Burnout

Burnout is an occupational phenomenon defined by the World Health Organization (WHO) as resulting from chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed. It is characterized by "feelings of energy depletion or exhaustion; increased mental distance from one’s job, or feelings of negativism or cynicism related to one's job; and reduced professional efficacy."

Exhaustion

Individuals experiencing burnout feel drained, tired, and overwhelmed, struggling to find the energy needed to meet the demands of their job and personal life. This fatigue can lead to reduced productivity and increased vulnerability to illness.

Depersonalization

This component involves a growing mental distance from one's job and a sense of negativity or cynicism towards it. It can manifest as a loss of enthusiasm, disconnection from colleagues or clients, and a general sense of disillusionment with the work environment. Individuals may become emotionally detached and less engaged in their tasks.

Inefficiency

This component relates to reduced professional efficacy, which means feeling ineffective and incompetent in one's role. People experiencing this aspect of burnout doubt their ability to perform well, which can erode their self-esteem and further contribute to burnout. It often results.
